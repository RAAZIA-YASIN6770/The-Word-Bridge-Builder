# Epics & User Stories: The Word-Bridge Builder

This document breaks down the "The Word-Bridge Builder" into actionable epics and stories following the BMAD methodology.

## ðŸŒ‰ Epic 1: The Core Bridge (MVP)
*Goal: Establish the fundamental spell-to-build mechanic.*

- **Story 1: Bridge Skeleton**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to see a bridge skeleton with missing letters,
  - **So that** I know which word I need to complete to cross.
- **Story 2: Letter Interaction**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to tap or drag letters into the bridge slots,
  - **So that** I can interact with the environment and solve the puzzle.
- **Story 3: Material Transformation**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** the bridge to turn into Steel (correct) or Paper (incorrect) based on my answer,
  - **So that** I get immediate periodic feedback on my spelling.

## ðŸ‘¤ Epic 2: Character & World (Levels 1-5)
*Goal: Create the narrative and physical consequences of gameplay.*

- **Story 4: Successful Crossing**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to see my character walk across the bridge successfully,
  - **So that** I feel a sense of accomplishment for solving the word.
- **Story 5: Bridge Collapse**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to see a 'Game Over' animation if the bridge collapses,
  - **So that** I understand the stakes of spelling incorrectly.

## ðŸ’¡ Epic 3: Assistance & Retention
*Goal: Provide help and progression systems to keep the player engaged.*

- **Story 6: Lightbulb Hint**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to use a 'Lightbulb' hint to see a picture of the word,
  - **So that** I can get past a word I'm struggling with.
- **Story 7: Star Progression**
  - **As a** player,
  - **I want** to earn Stars to unlock the next world,
  - **So that** I have a clear long-term goal to work towards.

## âœ… Definition of Done (DoD) for First Playable Prototype
The "First Playable" is considered done when:

1. **Functional Spelling:** A user can complete a 3-5 letter word using the tap/drag interface.
2. **Visual Feedback:** The bridge transforms into a "Solid" state (Steel) or "Fragile" state (Paper) immediately upon completion.
3. **Character Sequence:** The character attempts to walk across the resulting bridge.
4. **Win/Loss States:** The level ends with either a "Success" (crossing) or "Failure" (falling) trigger.
5. **Basic World:** At least one environment (e.g., The Forest) is rendered with basic assets.
